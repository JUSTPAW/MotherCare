    Gynecology (OB/GYN)

    Provide preventive care, diagnose and treat reproductive health issues, perform surgeries, and assist with childbirth and postpartum care.

    Gynecology is a branch of medicine that deals with the female reproductive system and includes a range of services, such as routine checkups, Pap tests, breast exams, family planning, menopause management, and treatment for reproductive health issues such as endometriosis, fibroids, sexually transmitted infections, and infertility. Gynecologists also perform surgeries such as hysterectomy, oophorectomy, and laparoscopy.

    Obstetrics

    Provide medical care for pregnant women, manage labor and delivery, perform cesarean deliveries, and offer postpartum care.

    Obstetricians are medical doctors who specialize in caring for pregnant women and managing childbirth. They provide prenatal care, monitor fetal development, manage labor and delivery, perform cesarean deliveries, and offer postpartum care. They may also manage high-risk pregnancies, such as those with pre-existing medical conditions or pregnancy complications.

    Frequently Asked Questioins

    Find answers to all your questions about pregnancy, childbirth, and postpartum care, from our team of experts who have compiled a list of frequently asked questions to help guide you through your pregnancy journey

    • What are the early signs of pregnancy?

      The most common early signs of pregnancy include missed period, fatigue, nausea, breast tenderness, and frequent urination.

    • How soon can a pregnancy test detect pregnancy?

      Most pregnancy tests can detect pregnancy as early as one week after a missed period, but it depends on the type of test used and the level of the pregnancy hormone (hCG) in your body.

    • What should I eat and avoid during pregnancy?

      During pregnancy, it's important to eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, and dairy. You should avoid certain foods that can be harmful to your baby, such as raw or undercooked meat, fish high in mercury, unpasteurized dairy products, and deli meat.

    • How much weight should I gain during pregnancy?

      The amount of weight you should gain during pregnancy depends on your pre-pregnancy weight and BMI. In general, women with a healthy BMI should aim to gain 25-35 pounds during pregnancy.

    • What exercises are safe to do during pregnancy?

      Most forms of low-impact exercise, such as walking, swimming, and yoga, are safe to do during pregnancy. It's important to talk to your healthcare provider before starting any new exercise routine during pregnancy.

    • What are the different stages of labor and delivery?

      Labor and delivery can be divided into three stages. The first stage is characterized by contractions and dilation of the cervix. The second stage involves pushing and delivery of the baby. The third stage involves delivery of the placenta.
